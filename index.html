Research

My research focuses on photonics and metamaterials, allowing us to better understand and use light-matter interactions. I currently work with Dr. Andrea Alù at the Advanced Science Research Center at the City University of New York.

I currently explore photonics in both the optical and radio frequency (RF) wavelengths, mainly in simulating, fabricating and characterising metamaterials: structures that have intended properties that largely arise from their often periodic and sub-wavelength synthetic structures. One of my research pathways explores time-varying optical metamaterials in pump-probe spectroscopy experiments. By altering the spatial and temporal profiles of ultrafast laser pulses, we can observe changes in the response of these metamaterials. I also investigate RF metamaterials for exploring other electromagnetic phenomena, such as negative refraction, complex frequencies and plasmonic cloaking.

While I am now an experimental physicist, my research aims were not always in physics. I began academic research in my first year of university, joining a microbiology lab to explore genetic engineering and synthetic biology. I began to get involved in analytical and interstellar chemistry research, where I then pivoted to physics and have stayed in various subfields of atomic, molecular and optical physics ever since.
